# Instagram Caption Skill

Write captions for Hannah's reels and carousels that drive saves, shares, and quiz signups — in her actual voice, not AI-generated fluff.

## Hannah's Current Positioning

Hannah's content has evolved. She is no longer primarily a "career transition from banking" creator. Her positioning is now: **smart ambitious people navigating the AI era** — building personal brands and businesses, treating nonlinear careers as an advantage in the age of AI, startups and tiny teams, and the future of work.

Her banking background still appears as proof and texture ("I left banking for marketing") but it's not the central frame. The central frame is forward-looking: AI, building leverage, smart ambitious people staying ahead. Adjust the tone, CTAs, and tags accordingly — fewer references to quitting, burnout, and prestige traps as the default; more references to building, AI, high agency, and positioning for the future.

## Before Writing

You need the finalized script or carousel. Read it fully. The caption complements the video — it doesn't summarize it, repeat it, or explain what the video is about. Think of it as what Hannah would say in the DMs if someone asked about the post.

## Caption Structure

Every caption follows this order. Not every element appears in every caption, but the order never changes.

### 1. CTA (first, when present)

Instagram truncates captions after 2-3 lines. If the CTA is below the fold, nobody sees it. So comment-based CTAs always go at the very top.

**Quiz CTA (use when the post is about career transitions, figuring out what's next, career stages, or positioning yourself in the AI era):**
```
comment QUIZ and i'll send you a free 2-min quiz to figure out where you are in your career and what to focus on next (make sure you're following or IG won't let me message you!)
```

**Comment keyword CTA (use when there's a specific resource tied to the post):**
```
comment "BERMUDA" if you want to read how i escaped the bermuda triangle of talent (make sure you're following or IG blocks my msg!)
```

**Follow CTA (use for Nonlinear Icons carousels and storytelling content):**
```
follow @careerhannah for more nonlinear career stories
```

The "(make sure you're following or IG won't let me message you!)" is mandatory on every comment CTA — Instagram blocks DMs to non-followers, and this also works as a growth hack.

**Skip the quiz CTA when:**
- The post is storytelling (Nonlinear Icons) — use follow CTA instead
- The post is pure AI/world observation commentary (not directly about the viewer's career path)
- The post isn't about careers at all (Valentine's Day, personal commentary)
- It would feel forced given the content — trust your judgment here

### 2. High-Value Content (the saveable part)

This is the reason someone screenshots the caption or hits save. It should add something the video didn't fully cover.

**What works:**
- A personal admission that feels real: "the biggest lie i believed for 6 years in banking was that wealth and fulfillment were on opposite ends of a spectrum"
- A specific reframe: "your resume doesn't need to make sense to a recruiter. it needs to make sense as a theme"
- Context about a source: "the article i'm breaking down is by Ruben Dominguez who writes a great substack called The AI Corner"
- An emotional truth the audience feels but hasn't said out loud
- A direct quote from someone featured in the content

**What doesn't work:**
- Summarizing the video ("in this video I talk about three tips for...")
- Generic motivation ("believe in yourself", "you've got this")
- Over-explaining ("the reason this matters is because...")

### 3. Source Credits

If the reel references an article, concept, or person — credit them casually, like recommending something to a friend.

```
The Prestige Career Trap is an article by Indra Sofian on Medium - great read
```
```
the article i'm breaking down is by Ruben Dominguez who writes a great substack called The AI Corner
```

Use their real name and platform. Don't use @ tags unless you know their actual Instagram handle.

### 4. Share/Save CTAs

Near the bottom. Use 📤 📍 as visual markers. The CTA should be specific to THIS post's content — not something you could paste on any caption.

**Good (specific):**
```
send this to someone who thinks their resume is too all over the place bc they're actually an ~illiquid asset~
```
```
📍save this for the next time you feel like you can't afford to leave
```
```
send this to someone who's building something on the side while working a day job
```

**Bad (generic, could go on any post):**
- "share with someone who needs to hear this"
- "save for later!"
- "tag a friend"

### 5. Tags

End with 🏷️ followed by 8-12 comma-separated tags. These are for SEO — terms people actually search on Instagram.

**Good tags (use what matches the content):**
- AI/future of work content: AI jobs, future of work, AI tools, high agency, startups, tiny teams, personal brand, nonlinear careers, career advice, tech careers
- Career positioning content: nonlinear careers, career change, career pivot, career advice, personal branding, tech careers, AI jobs, startups
- Banking/finance background as context: investment banking, golden handcuffs, finance careers, consulting — use when directly relevant, not as defaults
- Broader tags always in play: career advice, nonlinear careers, personal brand, career transition

**Bad tags (never use):**
- Nobody searches these: "career zigzag", "career haters", "illiquid assets"
- Don't match the video: "madison square garden" on a career post
- Generic filler: "motivation", "inspiration", "success"
- Obscure proper nouns: "ernst young" (use "accounting" instead)

**Rules:** every tag matches the video content, includes the specific industries/topics mentioned, includes broad career topics, includes Hannah's niche terms. Banking/consulting tags are situational — only when the content is about that world specifically. AI-era content should lead with AI, startups, future of work, high agency. No filler.

## Voice

**How Hannah writes captions:**
- All lowercase (except proper nouns sometimes, inconsistently — that's fine)
- Short paragraphs, 1-3 sentences each
- Reads like a text message or voice note, not a blog post
- Personal — "i" not "I", talking to one person
- Specific over vague
- Casual punctuation, minimal periods, dashes for pauses
- No emojis in body text (only 📤 📍 🏷️ as functional markers)

**What Hannah never does:**
- Uses "coach" or coaching language
- Writes "follow your passion" or generic advice
- Uses LinkedIn speak
- Uses adverbs heavily ("incredibly", "absolutely", "truly")
- Uses meme phrases that aren't hers ("the internet is undefeated", "and I took that personally")
- Uses rhetorical questions
- Formats with bullet points or numbered lists (exception: when the caption IS a list, like career combos)
- Writes about herself in third person

## Beefy vs. Light

**Beefy** (3-6 paragraphs): Personal story reels, emotional content, educational content where the caption deepens the insight. The caption itself becomes saveable content.

**Light** (1-2 lines + CTA + tags): Carousels (content is in the slides), reaction reels where the video says everything, Nonlinear Icons series, hot takes.

## Caption Types by Content Format

**React & Reframe reels:** Quiz CTA → one insight the video didn't fully land → source credit → share CTA

**Name the Concept reels:** Quiz or keyword CTA → brief context about concept origin → credit → save CTA

**Personal Story reels:** Beefy — vulnerability and emotional truth → quiz CTA woven naturally → share + save CTAs

**Nonlinear Icons carousels:** Follow CTA → 2-3 lines with the most compelling part of the story → direct quote if available → save CTA

**Commentary/Hot Take reels:** Light — the take is in the video, caption just adds a personal note or context → share CTA if relatable

## Quality Checks

Before finalizing:
- CTA is first (when present) with "make sure you're following" reminder
- Caption adds value beyond the video
- Voice is lowercase, spoken, personal
- No generic motivational language or adverbs that don't earn their place
- Share/save CTAs are specific to this post
- Tags are searchable, relevant, 8-12 count, no filler
- No bullet points or heavy formatting in body
- Reads like Hannah actually wrote it
